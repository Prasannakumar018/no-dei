A message to Tamil texters

No Dei

Don't text someone just "Dei" and wait for them to respond.

Just ask your question directly. Respect their time. It's not rude — it's actually more polite.

01

The Rule

Just. Ask. The. Question.

When you send someone Dei and nothing else, you're making them stop what they're doing, unlock their phone, respond, and then wait for you to continue.

They're held hostage. They can't help you until you decide to reveal what you actually wanted. It's like calling someone, hearing them pick up, and saying nothing — except slower and more annoying.

Instead, put your full message in one go: Dei, can you send me the notes from yesterday? — That's it. Done. Efficient. Respectful.

Dei, nee free ah irundha oru doubt iruku — can u help me debug this code? (pasting below)
ha da, send panu
🔗 [code snippet]

ADEI thank you da!!!
✅ Resolved in 4 minutes
02

Why it matters

01
Context switching is expensive
Every time someone checks a message and finds just "Dei", their brain has to switch gears, respond, and wait — interrupting deep work or focus multiple times for one simple thing.
02
Async is a gift — don't ruin it
Texting is great because people can respond when ready. But "Dei" forces synchronous behavior on an async medium. You're basically demanding they be online now.
03
Your friend isn't a customer service bot
Making someone do four back-and-forths just to reach the actual question wastes both your time. You already know what you want — just say it upfront, dei.
03

How to text properly

You can absolutely still use "Dei" — just don't stop there. Here are some good templates:

Asking for help
Dei,

nee free ah irundha oru doubt iruku. [problem description]. Nee help panna mudiyuma?

Sending something
Dei,

yesterday's notes file panutten, check pannu: [link/file]. Useful ah irukuma sollu.

Making plans
Dei,

Saturday 6pm la Marina ke varuviya? Dinner + beach walk plan iruku. Let me know!

Quick question
Dei,

exam date confirm ah? Naan 15th nu ketten but others 17th solaanga — which one is correct?
